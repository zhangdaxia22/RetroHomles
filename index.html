RetroHolmes: When Semantic Plausibility Fails Retrospective Physical Process Reasoning

RetroHolmes is a real-world benchmark that asks vision-language models to reason backward from outcomes: is a transformation physically reachable, and what causal steps could have produced it?

01 / The gap

A plausible procedure is not necessarily a possible one.

Modern VLMs can describe what a scene looks like and generate fluent instructions. But fluency can hide impossible physics: a chopped ingredient cannot become whole again, and a sealed container cannot be filled without opening it.

Can a model distinguish “sounds right” from “could really happen”?
TASK 01

World reachability judgment

Given two sparse visual endpoints, determine whether a valid physical transition exists.

TASK 02

Inverse step reconstruction

If reachable, infer the causal execution chain that bridges the initial and target states.

02 / RetroHolmes

Object-centric, process-oriented, deliberately diagnostic.

The benchmark moves beyond endpoint recognition and tests whether models can reason over hidden physical transitions.

Four transformation families.

RetroHolmes covers physical deformation, material and state change, topological assembly, and hybrid processes across cooking, woodworking, robotics, crafting, and other real-world domains.

Reachable / unreachableSparse endpointsCausal step chainsHuman verification
Five evaluation dimensions probe causal modeling, long-range reasoning, belief conflict, temporal reversibility, and irreversible micro-states.
03 / Simulate-and-Verify

Ground abstract reasoning in simulated state evolution.

A lightweight analysis-by-synthesis instantiation turns a verbal hypothesis into a visual trajectory that can be checked.
